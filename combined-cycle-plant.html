Combined Cycle Power Plants

Combined Cycle Plants are highly efficient, flexible, reliable, cost-effective and environmentally friendly solutions to generate electrical power.

In Combined Cycle Power Plants, DG set generates electricity while the waste heat from this DG set is used to make steam and in turn generate additional electricity via a steam turbine.

The flue gas heat is utilized to generate steam by passing it through a heat recovery boiler, which is used as input in the steam turbine to generate additional power. This dual cycle—DG and Steam Turbine—enhances the efficiency of the plant.

Simple cycle electrical efficiency typically ranges between 45% to 49%. Combined cycle mode increases efficiency up to 60%, contributing to low lifecycle costs and high operational flexibility.

Magnus Power offers a Combined Cycle Plant using world-leading Boiler (WHRB) technology from Aalborg and high-efficiency turbines to reduce the heat rate of the total Power Plant by up to 8%.

  • Aalborg boilers have over 20 years of seamless operation, approved by major engine manufacturers.
  • Turbines are proven and approved for use with Combined Cycle Boilers.
  • Plant can synchronize with the grid within 40-50 minutes, ideal for IPP operation.
